Pre-University Course

Arts/Humanities
PRE UNIVERSITY COURSE FOR ARTS
PUC - I Year:
Basic English is a compulsory language subject.
One more language is compulsory. Students can choose any one language of their choice from the following: Kannada, Hindi, Sanskrit, Marathi, Urdu, French
One of the following combinations of the subjects is to be chosen:
Group 1: History - Economics - Sociology - Political Science
Group 2: History - Economics - Logic - Sociology
Group 3: History - Economics - Sociology - Optional Kannada
Group 4: Economics - Sociology - Political Science - Psychology
Group 5: Economics - Logic - Sociology - Psychology
Group 6: History - Economics - Geography - Optional Kannada
Group 7: History - Economics - Geography - Political Science
Group 8: History - Economics - Optional Kannada - Hindustani Music
PUC - II Year:
Students of PUC II year Arts have to continue to study the same subjects which they choose in the first year of PUC.
Commerce

PRE UNIVERSITY COURSE FOR COMMERCE
PUC - I Year:
1. Basic English is compulsory
2. Another language to be chosen as a basic subject, from these: Kannada, Hindi, Marathi, Urdu and French
3. Any one of the following subject combinations should be chosen:
Group A: Business Studies, Accountancy, Statistics and Economics
Group B: Business Studies, Accountancy, Statistics and Basic Mathematics
PUC - II Year:
The students in PUC II year shall study the same subjects they choose in the first year.
Science
PCMB – Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics, Biology:
A combination of a science-oriented career. Most medical professionals and engineers start with these subjects. Those who are interested in doing research are also advised to choose this combination. Although there’s a chance of a list of options, it is also extremely challenging.
PCMC – Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics, Computer Science :
For students aspiring to become engineers, this is one of the best options because landing a job after PUC courses becomes easy. This combination is for those who have ruled out medicine as an option. This too is a challenging combination.
PCME – Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics, and Electronics:
Another great option for a career in engineering. For those of you inclined towards electronics, this combination preps you for engineering in the field of electronics.
